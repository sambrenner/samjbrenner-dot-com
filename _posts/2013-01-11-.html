Swype, an alternate keyboard for Android, has a neat feature where it will try and predict the next word I want to type by offering three suggestions. If you select a word, it will suggest three more. For example, I started by typing "this" and Swype suggested "was," "is," and "will."

I chose "was," and Swype then suggested "the," "a," and "not."

It also knows current affairs, kind of. I had some fun with titles: "President" brings up "Bush," "Barack," and "Obama." "Prime Minister" only suggests one name "Gordon," referring to Gordon Brown, Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 2007 to 2010. "Commissioner" brings up only one name, "Roger," referring to Roger Goodell, Commissioner of the NFL. But along with suggesting "Goodell," it suggests "Clemens" (the baseball pitcher) and "Federer" (the tennis player). "Roger" on its own, without "Commissioner," suggests "Ebert" instead of "Goodell."
