One assignment for DIY Health this week was to design two fantasy Quantified Self systems, one that focused on the body and one for something else. For the body, I chose to design a system for tracking back pain. Sensors on the feet and posterior track time spent sitting, standing and reclining. This combines with a user-supplied level of discomfort to identify trends in back pain.

For my non-body system, I chose to track social anxiety. Since it's something subjective (until a world in which we all have embedded brain sensors), I proposed a clicker-based system to discretely track feelings of social discomfort. Each click logs data such as where you are and who you are with to find patterns in anxious feelings.

This might be something worth revisiting, however.
