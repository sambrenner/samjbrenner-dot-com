This spring at ITP, I'll be taking Marina Zurkow's "Fungus Among Us." She's asked us to post a little bit about the specific topic we'd like to explore in the class, which for me is windowsill gardening. My windowsill has seen a few plants (both alive and dead) over the past few years and I've always told myself that eventually I'll put some effort into it and make a well-designed, complete garden.

After some preliminary research, there are a few ways I think fungi can help in a windowsill garden. Mycorrhizal fungi are fungi that live symbiotically with plant root systems, and I can explore ways to encourage their growth to benefit herbs, flowers and other plants I would want in my garden. This could involve research into the soil food web, which will also be relevant to the "Soil as Medium" class I'll be taking in the second half of this semester. For a more decorative approach, I can attempt to cultivate lichen or grow mushrooms out of logs, and perhaps attempt to make a fungal terrarium.
